About the Research Platform

This platform is a specialized firmware anti-forensics research and detection environment built for Betaflight-based drone flight controller firmware. It combines byte-level diffing, heuristic reasoning, stealth persistence discovery, rollback validation, secure boot trust analysis, fragmentation intelligence, and AI copilot explainability into a single workflow for forensic triage and academic demonstration.

Research Objective

The primary objective is to detect stealthy anti-forensic techniques hidden inside drone firmware images. These include covert implants, hidden telemetry channels, boot-stage persistence, rollback abuse, fragmented payload scattering, and disguised malicious metadata that may survive standard reflashing.

Detection Methodology

  • Binary diffing against trusted golden firmware
  • Heuristic mutation clustering and FF-fill analysis
  • Baseline profile deviation and stealth region checks
  • Rootkit persistence + syscall hook tracing
  • Secure boot, vector table, and reset integrity validation
  • AI copilot correlation for analyst explainability

Why Betaflight

Betaflight is widely used across FPV drones, autonomous UAV research, and embedded flight-controller ecosystems. This makes it an excellent case study for anti-forensic firmware tampering, where hidden modifications may alter PID loops, failsafe behavior, telemetry outputs, motor control, and mission logic.

Platform Workflow

1. Firmware Ingestion: Upload trusted and suspect Betaflight images.
2. Detector Correlation: Run all anti-forensics modules and entropy checks.
3. Threat Dashboard: Visualize severity, risk score, and module findings.
4. AI Copilot: Explain alerts, suggest validation steps, and remediation.
5. Forensic Reporting: Export analyst-grade PDF and JSON deliverables.
